# pysigQC Plots Guide

This document explains each plot generated by pysigQC, what it shows, and how to interpret it.

---

## Summary Radar Chart

**File:** `sig_radarplot.pdf`

A spider/radar chart summarizing all 14 QC metrics for each signature-dataset combination. Each polygon represents one signature in one dataset. Larger polygons indicate better overall signature quality.

### The 14 Metrics (counter-clockwise from top):

| # | Metric | Full Name | Interpretation |
|---|--------|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | Rel. med. SD | Relative Median SD | Signature variability vs all genes (higher = more variable) |
| 2 | Abs. skewness | Absolute Skewness Ratio | Distribution asymmetry (lower = more symmetric) |
| 3 | Top 10% CV | Prop. in Top 10% CV | Fraction of sig genes in top 10% variable genes |
| 4 | Top 25% CV | Prop. in Top 25% CV | Fraction of sig genes in top 25% variable genes |
| 5 | Top 50% CV | Prop. in Top 50% CV | Fraction of sig genes in top 50% variable genes |
| 6 | CV ratio | Coefficient of Variation Ratio | Signature CV vs all genes CV |
| 7 | Med. prop. non-NA | Median Proportion Non-NA | Data completeness (1.0 = no missing values) |
| 8 | Med. prop. expr. | Median Proportion Expressed | Fraction of samples expressing each gene |
| 9 | Autocorrelation | Intra-signature Correlation | How well signature genes correlate with each other |
| 10 | ρ(Mean,Med) | Spearman rho Mean vs Median | Correlation between mean and median scores |
| 11 | ρ(PCA1,Med) | Spearman rho PCA1 vs Median | Correlation between PC1 and median scores |
| 12 | ρ(Mean,PCA1) | Spearman rho Mean vs PCA1 | Correlation between mean and PC1 scores |
| 13 | Prop. var. PC1 | Proportion Variance PC1 | Variance explained by first principal component |
| 14 | Std. robustness | Standardization Robustness | Correlation between raw and z-transformed scores |

**Good signatures** typically have: high autocorrelation, high scoring metric correlations (ρ values), high PC1 variance, and good expression coverage.

## Variability Plots

**File:** `sig_mean_vs_sd.pdf`

Scatter plot of mean expression vs standard deviation for all genes.
- **Grey circles:** All genes in the dataset
- **Red circles:** Signature genes
- **Dashed lines:** Quantile reference lines (10/25/50/75/90 percentiles)

**Interpretation:** Good signatures have genes spread across the expression range, not clustered in low-expression/low-variance regions.

## Expression Plots

### NA Values Bar Chart
**File:** `sig_expr_barcharts_NA_values.pdf`

Bar chart showing the proportion of NA (missing) values for each signature gene, sorted descending.

**Interpretation:** Genes with high NA proportions may be problematic. Consider removing genes with >50% NAs.

### Expression Proportion Bar Chart
**File:** `sig_expr_barcharts.pdf`

Bar chart showing the proportion of samples where each gene is "expressed" (above threshold), sorted ascending.

**Interpretation:** Genes expressed in very few samples (<10%) may not contribute meaningful signal.

### Expression Density Plot
**File:** `sig_expr_density_plots.pdf`

Kernel density estimate of expression proportions across signature genes.

**Interpretation:** A peak near 1.0 indicates most genes are widely expressed. Bimodal distributions suggest heterogeneous gene behavior.

## Compactness (Autocorrelation) Plots

### Autocorrelation Heatmaps
**File:** `sig_autocor_hmps.pdf`

Clustered heatmaps showing pairwise Spearman correlations between signature genes.
- **Red:** Positive correlation (genes co-expressed)
- **Blue:** Negative correlation (genes anti-correlated)
- **White:** No correlation

**Interpretation:** Good signatures show warm colors (positive correlations). Blue patches indicate genes that may not belong together.

### Autocorrelation Density
**File:** `sig_autocor_dens.pdf`

Overlaid density curves of pairwise correlations for each signature-dataset.

**Interpretation:** Curves shifted right (toward +1) indicate tight co-expression. Curves centered at 0 suggest random gene sets.

### Rank Product Analysis
**File:** `sig_autocor_rankProd_<signature>.pdf`

Identifies genes with consistently low autocorrelation across datasets (potential outliers).

## Structure Plots

### Expression Heatmaps
**File:** `sig_heatmaps_hmps.pdf`

Hierarchical clustering heatmaps of signature gene expression across samples.
- Row dendrogram shows gene clustering
- Colors: blue (low) to red (high) expression

### Biclustering Heatmaps
**Files:** 
- `sig_biclust_<signature>_<dataset>.pdf` (continuous)
- `sig_biclust_binary_<signature>_<dataset>.pdf` (binarized)

Spectral biclustering results showing gene-sample blocks with coherent expression patterns.

## Scoring Metrics Comparison

**File:** `sig_compare_metrics_<signature>.pdf`

4-row layout per signature comparing different scoring methods:

| Row | Plot | Shows |
|-----|------|-------|
| 1 | Median vs Mean | 2D density scatter of sample scores |
| 2 | Mean vs PCA1 | Correlation between mean and PC1 scoring |
| 3 | PCA1 vs Median | Correlation between PC1 and median scoring |
| 4 | Scree plot | Variance explained by each principal component |

**Interpretation:** High correlations (ρ > 0.9) between scoring methods indicate robust signatures. Low PC1 variance (<50%) suggests the signature lacks a dominant expression pattern.

### QQ Plots
**File:** `sig_qq_plots_<signature>.pdf`

Quantile-quantile plots comparing score distributions to theoretical normal.

**Interpretation:** Points following the diagonal indicate normally distributed scores. Deviations suggest skewed or multi-modal distributions.

### Enrichment Score Comparisons
**File:** `sig_compare_ES_metrics_<signature>.pdf`

Pairwise comparisons of enrichment scoring methods (GSVA, ssGSEA, PLAGE).

---

## Standardization Comparison

**File:** `sig_standardisation_comp.pdf`

Scatter plot comparing raw median scores vs z-transformed median scores.

**Interpretation:** High correlation (ρ > 0.95) indicates the signature is robust to standardization. Low correlation suggests sensitivity to normalization method.

---

## Negative Control Plots

**File:** `sig_negative_controls.pdf`

Boxplots comparing actual signature metrics against null distributions from random gene sets.

- **Red triangles:** Actual signature values
- **Boxplots:** Distribution from permuted/random signatures

**Interpretation:** Actual values outside the null distribution boxes indicate the signature has properties unlikely to occur by chance.
